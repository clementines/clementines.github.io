Relationships between Mental Health, Lifestyle, and Health Outcomes

The visual to the right depicts attributes with the strongest positive correlation with an increased number of mentally unhealthy days per month. The packed bubbles are color-coded to a health outcome or a lifestyle attribute - blue for health issue; red for lifestyle. For example, there is a strong association between poor physical health days and poor mental health days. These are both health outcomes. Similarly, there is a strong relationship between adult smoking, a lifestyle attribute, and poor mental health.  These correlations indicate that health interventions related to mental health must consider both physical wellbeing as well as confounding lifestyle attributes.
